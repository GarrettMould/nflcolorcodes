Baltimore Ravens
When professional football returned to the city of Baltimore in 1996, both the name and colors were chosen as an ode to the city's history. Edgar Allen Poe, author of "The Raven", lived much of his life (and died) in Baltimore. The colors of purple, black, and gold were taken from Maryland's state flag, giving Baltimore one of the league's more unique color schemes.
